Case Study: Phishing Attack

Cybercrime Type: Phishing

Description:
Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers attempt to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or bank account details. This is usually done by impersonating a trusted organization through email, SMS, or fake websites.

How It Typically Happens:

The attacker sends a fraudulent email or message that appears to be from a legitimate source, such as a bank or online service.
The message often creates a sense of urgency, asking the recipient to “verify” account details or “reset” passwords.
The recipient clicks on a link, which leads to a fake website designed to look authentic.
The victim enters personal or financial information on the site.
The attacker collects the information and uses it for unauthorized transactions or identity theft.

Who Is Usually Targeted:
Phishing attacks can target anyone who uses online services, but students and professionals are common victims due to frequent email communication and online account usage.

Consequences:
Victims can suffer financial loss, identity theft, unauthorized account access, and reputational damage. In some cases, stolen credentials are sold on the dark web, putting victims at further risk. Organizations may also face data breaches and loss of trust from clients.

Realistic Scenario:
A student receives an email claiming to be from their bank, asking them to confirm their account details to prevent account suspension. Believing it to be genuine, they enter their credentials on the fake website. Soon after, unauthorized transactions appear in their account, and the student has to report the fraud to the bank and change all passwords.

Source: Adapted from general phishing attack reports by cybersecurity experts (e.g., Norton and Kaspersky).
